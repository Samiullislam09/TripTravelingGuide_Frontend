# Writing Rules — people-first, penalty-safe, snippet-ready

**The one job of every post: fully answer a real traveller's question better than
the current top results — so completely they don't need another tab.** Winning
the featured snippet / PAA is a *by-product* of being the clearest, most
trustworthy answer. It is never the goal.

Before writing, pass the **intent test**: *"Would I make this page, in this
depth, if Search did not exist?"* If the topic was picked mainly for traffic,
don't write it. We never write for the crawler or an AI detector — a clean
style/detector score is hygiene, never evidence of quality.

This file is *how to write*. `CONTENT_CHECKLIST.md` is the *publish gate*. Drafts
enter the dashboard as `pending_review` — **never auto-publish**; a named human
approves (drip ≤5/week).

*(Rules below reflect a 6-lens audit against Google's Helpful-Content, Quality
Rater Guidelines, spam policies, and rich-result docs — sources cited inline.)*

---

## 1. Experience — the first "E" (mandatory)

Every post must carry **at least one verifiable first-hand element**, sourced
honestly and **never invented**:
- **(a)** original photos/screenshots we actually took, with real captions; or
- **(b)** a named, quotable traveller/operator we spoke to (*"Priya, who sailed
  the Nov 2025 Kochi route, told us…"*); or
- **(c)** our own first-party data (a fare we were quoted **with the date**, a
  booking screenshot, a menu we photographed).

**If none exists, say so plainly** — this disclosure is *required*, not optional:
> *"This is a research-based guide built from official sources and verified
> traveller reports. We have not sailed this route ourselves."*

Never phrase *"expect roughly ₹X"* as lived experience when nobody experienced
it — downgrade it to a clearly-labelled, sourced explainer. *(Google 2022 E-E-A-T
update: demonstrated experience is the headline differentiator.)*

## 2. Trust & authorship — the pillar Google weights highest

**Site-level (must exist before any post is approved), linked in the footer:**
1. An **About page** naming the real people/entity behind TripTravelingGuide and
   why we cover travel.
2. **Working contact** (email/form).
3. A short **editorial & accuracy policy** (how we verify, how to report an error).

A strong article on an anonymous-looking site is a **Trust failure** and does not
get approved. *(QRG gives Lowest ratings to sites with no responsible-owner info.)*

**Byline:** must link to a real author page (`/author/<slug>`) with the author's
actual name, photo, relevant experience, and a `sameAs` to a real external
profile. Never invent an author or credentials. For AI-drafted + human-edited
posts, credit the accountable human: *"Reviewed by <real name>, <role>, on
<date>."*

**AI-use disclosure** (answers Google's "How"): a short honest process line near
the byline —
> *"Researched and drafted with AI assistance, then fact-checked and edited by
> <named human> against the official sources listed below on <date>."*

## 3. Facts, sourcing & originality (non-negotiable)

- **No fabrication.** Every price, name, policy = verified against the **official
  source**, cited, and dated.
- **Unconfirmed facts get flagged honestly**, not guessed: *"the cover charge
  isn't published — confirm at guest services onboard."* An honest gap is an
  E-E-A-T asset.
- **Attribute soft data**: *"travellers report ~₹350–500 a beer — confirm
  onboard, prices change."* Never present a blog number as official fact.
- **Two source types where relevant:** the **official** source for facts/prices,
  **plus ≥1 independent** corroborating source (reputable news, a tourism/
  government body, clearly-attributed traveller reports) — especially for
  reputation, safety, or disputed claims. On YMYL posts one vendor link is not
  enough. *(QRG reputation research relies on independent sources.)*
- **Real originality bar.** An "original element" must be something a reader
  **cannot get from the official source itself**: a first-hand observation, a
  price we confirmed (with date), an owned photo, primary data, a genuine
  hands-on comparison, or synthesis across sources none of them provides. A table
  that merely **reformats** the official page does **not** count — that's the
  "unoriginal/reworded" pattern the scaled-content spam policy names. If the post
  adds nothing beyond the official source + the current top 3, don't publish it.

## 4. YMYL track (prices, booking, refunds, visas, safety, health)

When in doubt, treat it as YMYL and apply the **strict track**:
1. Every money figure carries **"as of <month year>"** + a link to the official
   price source.
2. A visible line: *"Prices/policies change — confirm on the official site before
   you book or travel."*
3. Mandatory sign-off by the **named human reviewer**.
4. No blog-sourced number without the "confirm" caveat.

*(This site's staples — cruise fares, hidden charges, booking, refunds — are
financial-transaction content held to a "very high" PQ bar.)*

## 5. Snippet-ready FORMAT (the by-product, done right)

- **Answer in the first 40–55 words** under every `<h2>`/`<h3>`. Lead with the
  answer, then explain. Never bury it.
- **Question-style headings** matching real queries: *"Is food included on
  Cordelia Cruise?"* not *"Dining Inclusions."*
- **One comparison table**, plain headers (*Included* vs *Extra cost*). Tables
  win table-snippets.
- **Scannable lists** for anything 3+ items; short, uneven bullets.
- **A real FAQ** *only when readers genuinely have those follow-ups* — 5–8
  questions, each a real query answered in 40–55 words. Answer because it helps,
  not to hit a count.
- **Featured snippets & PAA are won by on-page content structure alone — NO
  schema influences them.** Do **not** expect an FAQ or HowTo rich result:
  Google restricted FAQ rich results to gov/health sites (Aug 2023) and removed
  HowTo entirely. Write step-by-steps as a plain ordered list. *(Never add schema
  expecting a snippet.)*

## 6. Human voice (write for the traveller, not a detector)

**Banned phrases / AI tells — never use:**
> "Whether you're… or…", "In today's world", "nestled", "boasts", "a testament
> to", "In conclusion", "Moreover / Furthermore", "It's worth noting", "delve",
> "elevate", "unlock", "seamless", "vibrant tapestry", "hidden gem",
> "picturesque", "when it comes to", "look no further", "rest assured",
> "Overall", "Ultimately", "That said", "Additionally", "In addition", "As a
> result", "Needless to say", "At the end of the day", "keep in mind", "as
> mentioned earlier".

**Never use an em dash (—).** Not the character, not `&mdash;`, not in any post,
heading, meta, caption or web story. It is the single loudest AI tell in our
copy, and we lean on it far too much. Rewrite the sentence instead:

| Instead of the em dash | Use |
| --- | --- |
| An aside mid-sentence | A comma pair, or brackets |
| A hard turn or reveal | A colon, or a full stop and a new sentence |
| A list gloss (`Starlight — the buffet`) | A verb: "Starlight **is** the buffet" |
| A range (`₹350—500`) | An en dash `&ndash;` or the word "to" |

En dashes in numeric ranges and ordinary hyphens are fine. Check before saving:
a draft with a single `—` or `&mdash;` in it fails the gate.

**Cut hedging & empty intensifiers.** Drop "can be / may / often / generally /
typically / tends to / a variety of / some travellers" — either state the fact,
attribute it with a real number/source, or admit you don't know (§3). Ban "truly
stunning / must-see / breathtaking" — replace with the fact that earns it (*"you
can see the Bandra–Worli Sealink from deck 11"*). Cap em-dashes at ~2 per post.

**Structural rhythm (biggest AI fingerprint):**
- Vary **paragraph** length like sentences — mix 4-sentence blocks with 1-line
  ones; ≥1 single-line paragraph per post; never 3 consecutive same-shape blocks.
- Break symmetry: no rule-of-three padding, no "not only… but also", uneven list
  items (a 2-word bullet beside a 15-word one reads human).
- **No warm-up intro** ("Planning a cruise can be overwhelming") — first sentence
  states the answer or a specific fact. **No recap conclusion** — end on the
  single most useful concrete thing (the deadline, the one tip, the number to
  call). Don't over-explain or define common terms.

**Take a position** (AI defaults to toothless, all-upside):
- Make ≥1 explicit recommendation and justify it (*"Skip the balcony cabin —
  you're barely in the room; put that into a shore excursion."*)
- Include ≥1 true point that **contradicts** the top pages or a common assumption
  (*"Most guides say buy the drinks package — do the math, at 2 drinks a day it
  loses money."*)
- Name ≥1 genuine **downside** and who should **not** go. All must be real and
  verifiable — never manufacture a fake contrarian take. A post that decides
  nothing is a rewrite candidate.

## 7. On-page technical

- **Title ≠ H1.** `<title>` (~50–60 chars) is for click-through: front-load the
  keyword, may add year/hook. **H1** (≤60 chars, exactly **one** per page) states
  the on-page promise plainly and contains the exact primary query. Never ship
  `<title>` == H1 verbatim. Schema `headline` ≤110 chars is a separate field.
- **Heading hierarchy** descends with no skipped levels (H3 only under an H2).
  Headings are structure, not styling.
- **Cover image (required).** Every post ships a real cover ≥1200px wide (16:9 +
  4:3 + 1:1 crops where possible) with descriptive alt **and** a caption. No
  cover = ineligible for the Article thumbnail, Top Stories and Discover — our
  main surviving rich-result path.
- **Internal links: 3–5 contextual, in-body** (not a footer dump): ≥1 **up** to
  the pillar/hub, 2+ **sideways** to sibling cluster posts; the pillar links
  **down** to the new post. No orphans. Descriptive anchors (*"Cordelia Cruise
  cabin fares"*, never "click here" or a raw URL); vary anchors, never repeat the
  exact-match money keyword as an anchor more than once.
- **Cannibalization check before writing.** Search the catalogue for the target
  query first. If a live post already targets it, **update that post**, don't
  publish a second. One primary query = one URL.
- **Slugs:** lowercase, hyphen-separated, 3–6 meaningful words, include the
  primary keyword, drop stop-words, **no year/date** in evergreen slugs. Once
  live the slug is **frozen** — changing it needs a 301. Match the migrated
  WordPress slug where one exists.
- **Meta:** title ~50–60 chars, description 140–155 chars — **pixel-budget
  guides, not guarantees** (Google truncates by width and often rewrites the
  description). Front-load keyword + hook. Every description unique.
- **Table of contents** on any post with 4+ H2s, jump-linking each H2; every H2
  has a stable human-readable `id` (`id="is-food-included"`) matching its wording.
- **Freshness honesty.** Advance "Last updated" / `dateModified` **only when the
  content materially changed** (re-verified prices, new sections, corrected
  facts) — never a cosmetic date/year swap. Note what changed. Visible date and
  schema date must always agree.
- **No cross-post boilerplate.** Intros/outros/disclaimers written fresh per
  post; if two drafts share >30% of sentences, merge them. Place the exact query
  in H1 + first sentence + slug + title + one H2, then **stop** repeating it —
  synonyms/entities elsewhere. No keyword-density target (that's a myth); delete
  any sentence that exists only to repeat the keyword.

## 8. Schema — allowed vs forbidden

- **Allowed:** `BlogPosting`/`Article` (with `author` Person + `sameAs`,
  `publisher` Organization, `datePublished`/`dateModified` matching the visible
  date, `image`), `BreadcrumbList`, `Organization`, `Person`.
- **Forbidden:** `Product`, `Offer`, `Price`, `Review`, `AggregateRating` — we
  don't sell cruises or host first-party reviews; marking up third-party prices
  or invented ratings is structured-data spam and risks a **manual action**.
  Also no `FAQPage`/`HowTo` as a ranking tactic (no rich result for us anyway).

## 9. Site-level (recovery is not just per-post)

Helpful-content/core signals are **site-wide**. Per-post perfection can't recover
the site while thin pages stay live. **Prune or noindex** thin, expired-seasonal,
fabricated, or pure-rewrite pages — a smaller genuinely-helpful site outranks a
large mostly-unhelpful one. A new good post does not offset an un-pruned bad one.

## 10. The gate (before approve in the dashboard)

Passes only if: **people-first intent test passed** • Who/How/Why answerable •
≥1 real first-hand element **or** the honest "we haven't been" disclosure • every
fact verified vs official source + dated • ≥1 **genuinely** original element •
≥1 explicit stance/recommendation • Trust infra live (About + contact + editorial
policy + real byline/author page) • YMYL track applied if money/safety • cover
image present • 3–5 contextual internal links, no orphan • no banned phrases •
**zero em dashes (`—` / `&mdash;`)** • no
forbidden schema • 700+ words of substance. **Draft-first `pending_review` —
never auto-publish.**
