## Read this before you re-open the "snow forecast" idea

The original ask was: *rank in Europe on snow-prediction keywords this winter, the
way we did ~2 years ago for ~3M visits.* Two independent research passes both said
don't. Recording why here so this doesn't get re-litigated in three months.

**1. The 3M almost certainly did not come from search.**
The entire English-language UK snow cluster runs an ESTIMATED 1-4M searches for a
whole Nov-Mar season, and ~85% of weather searches never click (Google answers in
the widget). The surviving clicks go mostly to Met Office and BBC. So the whole
market cannot produce 3M visits to one small site. The traffic pattern that *does*
fit is **Google Discover** (the phone news feed), and Discover traffic is exactly
the class the Helpful Content Update wiped out. **You cannot "rank" for Discover:
there is no keyword.** Planning to win it back via keywords is aiming at the wrong
target. *(Inference, not verified. See "How to settle this" below.)*

**2. "Europe" is not an English-language search market for weather.**
Germany searches `wetter` 37.2M/mo. The UK searches `weather` 30.4M/mo. Germany has
more people and out-searches the UK on weather **entirely in German**. English
`weather` does not appear in Germany's top-200 queries at comparable volume. The
pattern across Europe is: English for brand names, native language for information.
So "rank in Europe in English" really means **UK + Ireland only, ~73M people**, and
the UK has the **highest zero-click rate in Europe**. Wrong geography, wrong query
class.

**3. The seasonal-forecast SERP is a trap for us specifically.**
`europe winter forecast 2026 2027` looks winnable because the ranking domains are
weak (pogodnik.com, ugosnow.com, even dealsfor.me). What actually holds #1 is
**severe-weather.eu**: ~3,500 words, a named meteorologist, ECMWF/CFSv2/NMME model
charts. And **skiworld.co.uk**: 4,500 words behind a real ATOL-bonded booking
business. We have no meteorologist and no model data. **To compete we would have to
invent a forecast.** Seasonal forecasts are YMYL (people book travel and make safety
calls on them), we already carry 51 posts flagged for fabrication, and inventing
forecasts is precisely the scaled-fabrication pattern that caused the demotion.

**4. La Nina is over.** A strong El Nino is developing for 2026-27 (NOAA CPC
El Nino Watch, ~97% chance of continuing into early 2027). Any content built on a
La Nina premise for this season is **factually wrong on day one**. Our live post
`the-two-sides-of-the-winter-2025-26-forecast-la-nina-vs-the-almanacs` is about
*last* season and is fine, but do not extend that premise forward.

**What we do instead:** evergreen **winter travel climatology**. No forecasting, all
of it sourceable to historical data, and demonstrably held by travel blogs with no
more authority than us. It is travel content, which is our actual lane.

## Why this pocket is winnable (verified, not theory)

`will it snow in london` returns **europeinwinter.com, candaceabroad.com,
timelesstravelsteps.com, travellingmandala.com, boundlessroads.com**, an ecosystem
of personal travel blogs, none with meteorological credentials. On US-geo Google,
**firewoodfund.co.uk** (a firewood shop) ranks for UK winter predictions. That is
what a soft SERP looks like.

`does it snow in paris in december` (DuckDuckGo UK) returns **salutfromparis.com**,
a personal Paris blog. Same shape.

None of them forecast anything. They win on:
- **Historical climatology** ("London gets 16 snow days a year"), cited to Met
  Office / Weather Spark / Current Results
- **First-hand lived experience** (the 2018 Beast from the East, "I grew up here")
- **Travel utility**: what to wear, what to do, whether to go

**The decisive fact:** europeinwinter.com's `/snow-in-london/` is dated **29 Aug
2023** and still ranks **#3 in July 2026**. Evergreen climatology has **no timing
window and no republishing treadmill**. Write once, rank for years. This is the
opposite of the dated-forecast race, which we are already late for (unofficial
networks shipped "Winter Weather Forecast 2027" on 15 July 2026).

## About search volume: read once, then stop asking

**There are no real volume numbers in this file, because we have no tool that
produces them.** Volume comes from Ahrefs / SEMrush / Google Keyword Planner. We
have none of them. Any number written here would be invented, and inventing numbers
is what got this site demoted.

What the "Demand" column below actually means: **the query autocompletes on Google**
(pulled live, UK geo, 17 Jul 2026). Google only autocompletes strings people
actually type. That proves demand **exists**. It does not size it.

**To get real numbers, do these two free things:**
1. **Bing Webmaster Tools** is free and includes a keyword research tool with volumes.
2. **Google Search Console** is free. It is also the one place the real historical numbers
   might still exist (a property holds ~16 months). If an old GSC property from the
   WordPress era is still alive, it settles the 3M question outright.

Once either is live, rewrite this file against real data. Everything below is the
best plan available *without* data, which is strictly worse than a plan with it.

## The blocker nobody can code around

**Site-level HCU classification suppresses every page regardless of its quality.** A
winnable SERP does not mean we win it while classified. Prune + rewrite comes first;
these posts are the *content* half of the recovery, not a shortcut past it. Expect
months. (See `MEMORY.md` → SEO ranking audit.)

**And the honest gap in this plan:** the blogs that win this pocket win on real
lived experience. Candace lived in London a decade. If we cannot supply genuine
first-hand detail, we produce the same thin content that got us demoted and lose to
blogs that can. **Before writing any post below, answer: has anyone on this team
actually spent a winter in that city?**

- If **yes** → that city goes first. Real photos, real detail, real byline.
- If **no** → we still write it, but strictly as sourced climatology with the
  mandatory honest disclosure from `WRITING_RULES.md` §1 (*"This is a research-based
  guide built from official sources. We have not spent a winter in Paris."*), and we
  accept a lower ceiling. **Never fake the experience.**

## The posts, in publish order

Long-tail only, as requested. Each targets one primary query, question-shaped, with
month-level modifiers that are where the real long-tail demand sits.

### 1. Does it snow in London? (`does-it-snow-in-london`)

- **Primary:** `does it snow in london`
- **Demand:** VERIFIED autocomplete, plus the month long-tail: `...in december`,
  `...in november`, `...in january`, `...at christmas`, `...every year`, `...in may`
- **Competition:** travel blogs (europeinwinter #3 with a 2023 page, candaceabroad),
  netweather.tv, plus a firewood retailer. **No authority site owns it.**
- **Angle:** the honest answer is "less than you think, and rarely settles". This
  contradicts the fantasy the query is really asking about, which satisfies
  `WRITING_RULES.md` §6 (take a position, contradict the common assumption).
- **H2s:**
  - Does it snow in London? (answer in the first 40-55 words)
  - How many days does it snow in London each year?
  - Which months does it actually snow in London?
  - Does it snow in London at Christmas?
  - Does London snow settle, or just melt?
  - When did it last properly snow in London?
  - What to pack for London in winter
  - Frequently Asked Questions
- **Must cite:** Met Office historical averages, Weather Spark. Every number dated.
- **Original element required:** a month-by-month snow-day table built from Met
  Office data that no single source presents that way. A table that just reformats
  one official page does **not** count (§3).

### 2. Does it snow in Paris in December? (`does-it-snow-in-paris-in-december`)

- **Primary:** `does it snow in paris in december`
- **Demand:** VERIFIED autocomplete; the whole `does it snow in paris in [month]`
  family autocompletes (november, january, february, winter, at christmas).
- **Competition:** salutfromparis.com (personal blog). Soft.
- **Angle:** December in Paris is grey and wet, not white. Say so plainly.
- **H2s:** mirror post 1's shape, Paris-specific. Meteo-France + Weather Spark data.

### 3. Where to see snow in Europe in December (`where-to-see-snow-in-europe-december`)

- **Primary:** `best place to see snow in europe in december`
- **Demand:** VERIFIED autocomplete; also `where to see snow in europe december`,
  `best time to see snow in europe`, `best places to visit for snow in europe`.
- **Competition:** listicles. Beatable on specificity, not on being another listicle.
- **Angle:** this is the money query of the three, because it has **trip-planning intent**,
  not curiosity intent. Reader is deciding where to spend money.
- **Requirement:** must be a *decision* post, not a list of 15 places. Rank them,
  name who each is wrong for, and pick one. A post that decides nothing is a rewrite
  candidate (§6).
- **Cannibalization watch:** do not let this overlap posts 1 and 2. This one answers
  *where to go*; those answer *what a given city is like*. Link between them.

## Timing

Evergreen climatology has **no deadline**, which is the entire point of choosing it
over the forecast race. But readers plan winter trips from roughly September, so:

- **Aug 2026:** draft posts 1-3, human fact-check against Met Office / Meteo-France.
- **Sep 2026:** publish at the drip rate (max 5/week, per `WRITING_RULES.md`).
- **Oct 2026 onward:** these keep ranking with no republish. Do not touch the dates
  cosmetically; advance `dateModified` only on a real content change (§7).
